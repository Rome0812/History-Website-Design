Pahiyas Festival
(Lucban, Quezon) The Pahiyas Festival is a vibrant and colorful celebration held in Lucban, Quezon, typically on May 15th, to honor San Isidro Labrador, the patron saint of farmers. During the festival, houses in the town are adorned with colorful decorations made from agricultural produce such as rice, fruits, and vegetables. The most iconic decoration is the kiping, leaf-shaped rice wafers dyed in various bright colors, which are arranged in intricate patterns. The festival is not only a visual feast but also a reflection of the town's gratitude for a bountiful harvest. Visitors can enjoy lively street performances, traditional music, and local delicacies, making it a joyous celebration of Filipino culture and agricultural abundance.
Panagbenga Festival
(Baguio) also known as the Baguio Flower Festival, is an annual event held in Baguio City, the summer capital of the Philippines. The festival, which typically takes place in February, is a celebration of the blooming season and a showcase of the city's vibrant floral heritage. The highlight of the festival is the Grand Street Parade, where participants adorned in elaborate flower-themed costumes dance through the streets to the beat of lively music. Another popular event is the Float Parade, featuring floats adorned with colorful flowers of various shapes and sizes. The festival also includes various cultural shows, exhibitions, and trade fairs, making it a lively and colorful celebration that attracts visitors from all over the country
Higantes Festival
(Angono, Rizal) The Higantes Festival is a colorful and lively celebration held in Angono, Rizal, usually on November 22, the feast day of St. Clement, the patron saint of fishermen. The festival features giant papier-mâché effigies, known as "higantes," which can reach up to 12 feet in height. These effigies represent caricatures of Spanish colonial landlords and are paraded through the streets of Angono amidst music, dancing, and revelry. The festival has its roots in the Spanish colonial era when Angono's residents used the higantes to mock and criticize their oppressive landlords. Today, the Higantes Festival has become a symbol of Angono's cultural heritage and a colorful expression of Filipino creativity and resilience.
Giant Lantern Festival
(San Fernando, Pampanga) also known as the Ligligan Parul, is an annual event held in San Fernando, Pampanga, often called the "Christmas Capital of the Philippines." The festival showcases gigantic lanterns, locally known as "parol," which are intricately designed and illuminated with thousands of lights. Each lantern can reach up to 20 feet in diameter and is traditionally made from bamboo and colorful Japanese paper. The festival is a competition among barangays (villages) to create the most elaborate and visually stunning lantern. The highlight of the festival is the Grand Lantern Parade, where the giant lanterns are paraded through the streets, accompanied by music and dance performances. The Giant Lantern Festival has become a symbol of Filipino creativity, ingenuity, and the spirit of Christmas.
Moriones Festival
(Marinduque) The Moriones Festival is a unique and colorful celebration held in the island province of Marinduque, typically during Holy Week, leading up to Easter Sunday. The festival reenacts the story of Longinus, a Roman centurion who pierced the side of Jesus Christ with his spear during the Crucifixion. According to tradition, Longinus was blind in one eye, but when the blood of Jesus touched his eye, he was healed and converted to Christianity. The "Moriones" refers to the Roman soldiers, dressed in colorful costumes and masks, who portray Longinus and his fellow soldiers searching for him. The festival includes street performances, processions, and pageants, culminating in a dramatic reenactment of Longinus' conversion. The Moriones Festival is not only a religious observance but also a cultural tradition that attracts tourists from around the world.
