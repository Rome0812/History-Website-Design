Notable

Nur Misuari

In this Philippine name, Nur Misuari (Tausug: Nūr Miswāri, born Nurallaji Pinang Misuari; March 3, 1939) is a Moro Filipino revolutionary and politician, founder and leader of the Moro National Liberation Front. The middle name, or maternal family name, is Pinang, and the surname, or paternal family name, is Misuari.

Notable

Hidilyn Diaz

Born on February 20, 1991, Hidilyn "Hidi" Francisco Diaz-Naranjo is a Filipino weightlifter and airwoman. Her accomplishment in the women's 55 kg weightlifting category at the 2020 Summer Olympics has earned her two Olympic records. The first-ever gold medal for the Philippines was won by Diaz on July 26, 2021, during the women's 55 kg weightlifting competition at the 2020 Summer Olympics. She also set records for the 55 kg division in the clean and jerk, weighing 127 kg, and the total, weighing 224 kg.

Notable

Manny Pacquiao

Manny Pacquiao, a professional boxer, media personality, and politician, was born in Kibawe, Bukidnon province, Mindanao, Philippines, on December 17, 1978. He rose to prominence by becoming the most successful boxer in history to win titles in a record number of weight classes. His life outside the ring added to the incredible story of his ascent from extreme poverty to the top of his sport. In the Philippines, where he achieved unparalleled popularity through commercial endorsements, movies, television series, CDs, and his image on a postage stamp, the charismatic "Pac-Man" served as both an idol and a unifying force.

Notable

Antonio Bautista

(September 17, 1937 – January 11, 1974) was a pilot in the Philippine Air Force who flew the F-86 Sabre. He participated in the Blue Diamonds aerobatic display team and engaged in combat with rebels in the southern region of the nation. He was slain in January 1974 while on the job.

Notable

Vicente Alvarez Dizon

Born in Malate, Manila, on April 5, 1905, Vicente ALvarez Dizon lived from 1905 to 1947. Jose Sampedro Dizon, a native of Bacolor, and Rosa Carlos Alvarez, a resident of Concepcion, Tarlac, were his parents. His father worked for the Bureau of Agriculture as an agronomic and landscape artist, having graduated from University of Santo Tomas in 1897. Mr. Jose S. Dizon worked as an agricultural inspector and agronomist in a number of places, including Capas, Tarlac, Magalang, Pampanga, and San Isidro, Nueva Ecija. Vicente attended Malate Primary School for his early education before pursuing his intermediate education in the locations where his father was stationed.
